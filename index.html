Madan Timalsina, Ph.D.

NESAP for Data Postdoctoral Fellow
NERSC & Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory
Berkeley, CA

I am a postdoctoral fellow in the NERSC NESAP for Data program within the Data and AI Services group at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. My work focuses on performance engineering, workflow modernization, and scalable scientific computing for data-intensive applications on leadership-class HPC and cloud-based systems. I am experienced in GPU acceleration, parallel I/O, containerized software environments, checkpoint/restart workflows, and orchestration using tools such as SLURM, CI/CD pipelines, and the NERSC Superfacility API.

As a NESAP postdoc, I collaborate with NERSC staff, vendors, and domain scientists to improve the performance, portability, and reliability of simulation, data analytics, and AI/ML workflows across DOE science projects, including the DUNE neutrino experiment and the LZ dark matter experiment.

I earned my Ph.D. in nuclear and particle physics from the South Dakota School of Mines and Technology in 2022. My doctoral research with the LUX-ZEPLIN dark matter experiment focused on detector calibration, simulation, and analysis workflows to improve the search for dark matter.

Research

DUNE · GPU Optimization

GPU Performance Engineering for LArTPC Neutrino Simulation

Led end-to-end GPU optimization of the DUNE larnd-sim workflow on NERSC Perlmutter (NVIDIA A100), targeting algorithmic, memory, and I/O bottlenecks identified via NVIDIA Nsight Systems/Compute and Darshan/Drishti.

  • >5× end-to-end runtime reduction
  • ~4× GPU memory savings through buffer reuse and kernel tuning
  • Ported and benchmarked across A100 (Perlmutter) and GH200 (TACC Vista)
  • Improved parallel I/O using local staging, sbcast, and DVS mounts
  • Mentored GPU hackathon team; achieved up to 5× kernel speedups
LZ · Data Pipeline

High-Throughput Dark Matter Detector Data Processing

Scaled and modernized the LZ Prompt Processing pipeline for near-real-time detector data processing at NERSC, supporting sustained multi-TiB/day throughput for the LUX-ZEPLIN dark matter experiment.

  • ~30% improvement in production throughput
  • Sustained >1 GB/s processing with near-real-time monitoring
  • Migrated workflow storage from CFS to Lustre scratch for I/O gains
  • Integrated SLURM, RabbitMQ, Spin, and NERSC Superfacility API
HPC Infrastructure

Containerized Checkpoint/Restart for Long-Running HPC Jobs

Designed and built containerized checkpoint/restart workflows using DMTCP, enabling resilient and portable long-running jobs across DOE HPC systems. Published at CUG 2024.

  • Portable across Docker, Shifter, Podman, and Apptainer/Singularity
  • SLURM-integrated for automated periodic checkpointing under walltime limits
  • Adopted by multiple science teams across DOE systems
  • arXiv:2407.19117
AI/ML · HPC Operations

Agentic & RAG Workflows for HPC User Support

Co-developed ATHOS, a model-agnostic agentic toolkit for HPC operations and user support, evaluated on NERSC-derived knowledge and troubleshooting scenarios. Built RAG and agentic evaluation pipelines and benchmarked LLM inference stacks for interactive HPC support workflows.

  • Benchmarked Dynamo vs vLLM vs SGLang on throughput, prefill, decode, and latency
  • Measured consistency, retrieval quality, and failure modes across support tasks
  • Automated GPU/data workflows with SLURM, ClearML, and CI/CD pipelines
  • Published at CUG 2026 (Nice, France)
Ph.D. Research · LZ

Detector Calibration & Simulation for Dark Matter Search

Doctoral research within the LUX-ZEPLIN (LZ) collaboration, focused on calibration methods, detector simulation, and analysis workflows to improve sensitivity in the search for WIMP dark matter.

  • Built and validated GEANT4-based neutron and gamma-ray simulation pipelines
  • Maintained core LZ software: BACCARAT, DER, and LZap
  • Led electron lifetime and purity tracking for detector-quality monitoring
  • Contributed to LZ first results (PRL 2023) and background determination (PRD 2023)
